=== CAT / MBA EXAM OVERVIEW ===
Full form: Common Admission Test
Conducting body: IIMs (rotational — different IIM hosts each year)
Purpose: Admission to MBA/PGDM at IIMs and 1200+ B-Schools
Frequency: Once a year (last Sunday of November)
Total attempts: No limit
Age limit: No upper age limit
Eligibility: Bachelor's degree with minimum 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/PwD)
Total IIM seats: ~5,000+ across all IIMs
Total MBA seats accepting CAT: ~4,00,000+

=== CAT EXAM PATTERN ===
Section 1 - VARC (Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension):
- 24 questions, 40 minutes
- RC passages: 4-5 passages with 3-4 questions each
- VA: Para Jumbles, Para Summary, Odd Sentence Out
- MCQ + TITA (Type In The Answer) mix

Section 2 - DILR (Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning):
- 20 questions, 40 minutes
- DI sets (graphs, tables, caselets) and LR sets (arrangements, puzzles)
- 4-5 sets with 4-5 questions each
- Known as the most unpredictable section

Section 3 - QA (Quantitative Ability):
- 22 questions, 40 minutes
- Arithmetic, Algebra, Number Systems, Geometry, Mensuration, Combinatorics

Total: 66 questions, 198 marks, 2 hours
Negative marking: -1 for wrong MCQs; No negative for TITA
Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

=== CAT SYLLABUS ===
VARC: Reading Comprehension, Para Jumbles, Para Summary, Odd Sentence Out
DILR Data Interpretation: Tables, Bar Graphs, Pie Charts, Line Graphs, Caselets
DILR Logical Reasoning: Seating Arrangements, Blood Relations, Puzzles, Games, Tournaments
QA Arithmetic: Percentages, Profit-Loss, SI/CI, Time-Speed-Distance, Time-Work, Ratios
QA Algebra: Equations, Inequalities, Functions, Progressions
QA Number Systems: Divisibility, Remainders, HCF/LCM, Factors
QA Geometry: Triangles, Circles, Coordinate Geometry, Mensuration
QA Combinatorics: Permutations, Combinations, Probability

=== CAT STRATEGY ===
Timeline: 6-12 months of dedicated preparation
For working professionals: 4-6 hours daily for 8-10 months

Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Build fundamentals — reading habits, math basics, DI/LR sets
Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Intensive practice — 200+ questions per QA topic, daily RC
Phase 3 (Months 7-9): Weekly full-length mocks with thorough analysis
Phase 4 (Last 2 months): 2-3 mocks per week, time management optimization

Key Tips:
- Reading habit is non-negotiable for VARC — read daily for 1 hour
- In DILR, set selection is crucial — skip the toughest set
- Mock test analysis is MORE important than taking the mock
- CAT tests speed and accuracy equally

=== COMMON CAT QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS ===
Q: Can I prepare for CAT while working full-time?
A: Absolutely. Many CAT toppers are working professionals. Work experience (2-4 years) is valued by IIMs. Dedicate 3-4 focused hours daily. Weekends for full mocks. Being systematic is key.

Q: What percentile is needed for IIM?
A: Top old IIMs (A,B,C) need 99+. New IIMs need 92-97. But CAT score is only 30-40% of selection — academics, work experience, diversity, and PI matter too. A 97 percentile with good work experience can beat 99 at some IIMs.

Q: How to improve RC for CAT?
A: Read diverse content daily — newspapers, The Economist, non-fiction books. Practice active reading. Solve 3-5 passages daily. CAT RC tests understanding of tone and argument structure, not fact memorization.

Q: Is CAT tougher than other MBA exams?
A: CAT is tougher than SNAP, CMAT, MAT but comparable to XAT. DILR section is unique and challenging. Good CAT prep automatically prepares you for most other MBA exams.

Q: Do IIMs consider 10th and 12th marks?
A: Yes! Most IIMs include 10th, 12th, and graduation marks in shortlisting. For IIM-A, academics carry about 25-30% weight. Weak academics need higher CAT percentile to compensate.

Q: What is the ROI of MBA from IIM?
A: Top IIM graduates earn Rs 25-40 LPA average starting salary. Fee is Rs 20-25 lakhs for 2 years. Most recover investment in 1-2 years. Even newer IIMs offer 12-18 LPA average. MBA from a good B-School has among the highest ROI in education.

Q: How many mock tests should I take?
A: Minimum 25-30 full-length mocks over 4-5 months. But analysis is more important than quantity. Track accuracy, time per question, and section-wise trends. Spend equal time analyzing as taking each mock.

Q: What is WAT (Written Ability Test)?
A: WAT replaced GD at most IIMs. You get a topic and 15-20 minutes to write a structured essay (250-300 words). Topics are abstract, social, or economic. Shows structured thinking.

Q: When to start CAT preparation?
A: Ideally 8-10 months before the exam. With strong quant/verbal background, 5-6 months can suffice. Starting in January-March for November CAT is ideal timing.

Q: Can I crack CAT without coaching?
A: Yes, many toppers are self-prepared. However, coaching provides structured guidance, mock tests, and peer benchmarking. At minimum, a good test series is essential.
